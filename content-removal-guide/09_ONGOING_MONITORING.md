# Phase 6: Ongoing Monitoring

Content frequently gets re-uploaded after removal. Setting up ongoing monitoring ensures you catch and take down re-uploads quickly, before they spread.

---

## Free Monitoring Methods

### 1. Google Alerts

Set up automated email notifications whenever your name/stage name appears in new Google search results.

1. Go to: https://www.google.com/alerts
2. Create alerts for each of the following (one alert per search term):
   - `"YourStageName"` (in quotes for exact match)
   - `"YourStageName" cam` or `"YourStageName" webcam`
   - `"YourRealName"` (only if publicly associated with your content)
   - Any known **misspellings** of your stage name
3. Settings for each alert:
   - **How often**: As-it-happens
   - **Sources**: Automatic
   - **Language**: Any (or specify Russian + English)
   - **Region**: Any
   - **How many**: All results
   - **Deliver to**: Your email address
4. Review alerts as they come in. If a new instance appears, immediately file a takedown using the process in Phase 2.

### 2. Periodic Manual Searches

Set a **recurring calendar reminder** (weekly or biweekly) to run the same searches from Phase 1:

- [ ] Google: `"YourStageName"`
- [ ] Yandex: `"YourStageName"`
- [ ] Bing: `"YourStageName"`
- [ ] Google Images: reverse image search with your photo
- [ ] Yandex Images: reverse image search
- [ ] TinEye: reverse image search
- [ ] Direct searches on major adult sites (Pornhub, XVideos, XHamster, etc.)

### 3. TinEye Alerts

TinEye offers free alerts for reverse image monitoring:

1. Go to: https://tineye.com/
2. Upload a distinctive photo of yourself
3. TinEye will notify you when new matches appear online

### 4. Social Media Monitoring

- **Reddit**: Search `"YourStageName"` periodically on Reddit search
- **Twitter/X**: Set up a saved search for your stage name — Twitter will show new results
- **Telegram**: Periodically search public channels for your stage name using Telegram's global search

## Preventive Measures for Future Streams

### Watermarking

Adding a visible or invisible watermark to your streams makes ownership claims easier:

- **Visible watermark**: Your stage name or a logo overlaid on the stream. Most cam platforms let you add overlays via OBS or their built-in tools.
  - In **OBS Studio**: Add a Text source or Image source with your watermark, position it on your stream
  - Keep it small enough not to distract viewers but large enough that it's visible in recordings
- **Invisible/digital watermark**: Embeds metadata that isn't visible but can be detected later. Tools like Digimarc or open-source alternatives can do this, though it's more complex.

### Stream Recording Settings

- Some cam platforms let you **disable recording by viewers** or restrict certain features
- Check your platform's broadcaster settings for options like:
  - Disable screenshots
  - Disable screen recording detection
  - Private/password-protected shows (harder to record and redistribute)
- **Note**: These are not foolproof — determined users can still capture screens. But they reduce casual recording.

### StopNCII.org Hashing

Register hashes of your content proactively:

1. Go to: https://stopncii.org/
2. Create hashes of your images and distinctive screenshots from your streams
3. Participating platforms will automatically detect and block matching uploads
4. This is **preventive** — do it before content spreads

## When You Find New Content

When monitoring reveals a new upload:

1. **Document it** — add to the tracking spreadsheet with screenshot and URL
2. **File immediately** — use the DMCA template (already drafted) and send to the platform
3. **File for de-indexing** — submit to Google/Bing/Yandex in parallel
4. **Report via NCII channels** if available on that platform
5. **Track the removal** in your spreadsheet
