# Phase 1b: Proving Original Content Ownership

To file a successful DMCA takedown, you need to demonstrate that **you are the copyright owner** of the recorded content. Cam performers automatically own the copyright to their live performances. This guide explains what proof to gather.

---

## What You Own

As a webcam performer, you hold copyright over:

- Your **live-streamed performances** (these are audiovisual works protected by copyright law)
- Your **likeness and image** in those recordings
- Any **photos or videos** you created and uploaded yourself

You do **not** need to have formally registered the copyright. Copyright exists automatically from the moment the work is created (under the Berne Convention, which Russia has signed).

---

## Proof to Collect

### 1. Platform Account Verification

- [ ] **Screenshot your profile page** on each cam platform you use (Chaturbate, StripChat, MyFreeCams, BongaCams, etc.)
  - Show your username, profile photo, and account creation date
- [ ] **Screenshot your broadcaster dashboard** showing streaming history, dates, and earnings
- [ ] **Export your payout history / earnings reports** if the platform allows it (this proves you were actively streaming on specific dates)
- [ ] **Screenshot or save the account registration confirmation email** from each platform

### 2. Platform Terms of Service

Most cam platforms explicitly state that the performer retains copyright. Save the relevant sections:

- [ ] **Chaturbate**: Terms state broadcasters own their content
- [ ] **StripChat**: Performers retain intellectual property rights to their streams
- [ ] **MyFreeCams**: Model agreement confirms content ownership
- [ ] Save a screenshot or PDF of the relevant Terms of Service section for your platform

### 3. Identity Verification

Platforms and legal processes may require proof that you are who you claim to be:

- [ ] A copy of the **government-issued ID** you used to verify on your cam platform (you can redact sensitive info like ID number — the key is matching your name to the account)
- [ ] The **model verification / age verification** confirmation from your platform (most platforms issue this when you sign up as a broadcaster)

### 4. Original Content Samples (if available)

- [ ] Any **recordings you made yourself** of your own streams
- [ ] **Photos you uploaded** to your profile that also appear on the infringing sites
- [ ] **Metadata** from original files (right-click > Properties/Get Info) showing creation date, device, etc.

---

## How This Proof Is Used

| Situation | What You Need |
|---|---|
| Filing a DMCA takedown with a website | Platform account screenshot + statement of ownership |
| Filing with Google/Bing for de-indexing | Same as above; Google may also ask for ID |
| Escalating to a hosting provider | DMCA notice + account proof + optionally a copy of platform ToS |
| Hiring a removal service | They will ask for your platform username, ID verification, and authorization letter |
| Legal action | All of the above, plus potentially a notarized statement |

## Authorization Letter (if using a representative)

If someone else (a lawyer, agent, or service) will file DMCA notices on your behalf, you should sign an authorization letter. A template is provided in `04_DMCA_TEMPLATE.md` under "Appendix: Authorization Letter."

---

## Storage and Security

- Keep all proof documents in an **encrypted folder** or password-protected archive
- **Do not share** your government ID more broadly than necessary
- When sending ID to platforms, use their **official verification channels** — never email unencrypted ID photos
