# Phase 4: Platform-Specific Non-Consensual Content Reporting

Many platforms have dedicated reporting mechanisms specifically for **non-consensual intimate images (NCII)** that are separate from standard DMCA. These are often faster and do not require disclosing your real name/address in a public DMCA filing.

Use these **in addition to** DMCA notices, not instead of them.

---

## What Is NCII Reporting?

Non-consensual intimate image (NCII) policies exist on most major platforms. They differ from DMCA in key ways:

| | DMCA Takedown | NCII Report |
|---|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Copyright infringement | Privacy / dignity violation |
| **What you prove** | You own the copyright | You are the person depicted and did not consent |
| **Public record** | DMCA notices can become semi-public | NCII reports are confidential |
| **Your identity** | Requires full legal name and address | Often requires only email + photo ID (kept private) |
| **Speed** | 1-7 days | Often 24-48 hours or faster |

**Recommendation**: File NCII reports where available (faster, more private), AND file DMCA notices (stronger legal footing).

---

## StopNCII.org (Cross-Platform Tool)

Before filing with individual platforms, consider using **StopNCII.org** — a free tool backed by Meta, TikTok, Reddit, Bumble, and others.

1. Go to: https://stopncii.org/
2. The tool creates a **digital fingerprint (hash)** of your images/videos on your own device — the actual content is never uploaded
3. Participating platforms automatically detect and block matching content
4. You receive a case number to track removals

**Limitations**: Not all adult sites participate, but it covers major social media and is worth doing early.

## Platform-by-Platform NCII Reporting

### Reddit

- **NCII Report**: https://reddit.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/requests/new?ticket_form_id=16374486893588
- Select "Non-consensual intimate media"
- Provide the post/comment URLs and a brief statement
- Reddit will remove and may ban the posting account
- Also report to subreddit moderators via modmail

### Twitter / X

- **NCII Report**: https://help.twitter.com/forms/private_information
- Select "An intimate photo or video of me or someone I'm authorized to represent"
- Provide the tweet URLs
- X will remove the content and may suspend the account
- Processing: Usually within 24 hours

### Facebook / Instagram (Meta)

- **NCII Report**: https://www.facebook.com/help/contact/567360146613371
- Or use StopNCII.org (Meta is a participating partner)
- For Instagram: https://help.instagram.com/contact/504521742987441

### TikTok

- **In-app**: Long press video > Report > "Nudity or sexual activity" > "Non-consensual intimate imagery"
- **Web form**: https://www.tiktok.com/legal/report/privacy
- TikTok participates in StopNCII.org

### Discord

- **Report form**: https://dis.gd/request — select "Trust & Safety"
- Include server name, channel, message links
- Discord removes NCII and bans the user

### Telegram

- **Email**: abuse@telegram.org
- **In-app**: Long press message > Report
- For channels: Open channel info > Report
- Include direct links to messages/media
- **Note**: Telegram can be slow to respond. Persistent, repeated reporting helps.

### Pornhub (Aylo network)

- **Content Removal**: https://www.pornhub.com/content-removal
- Also has a **non-consensual content** category separate from standard DMCA
- Requires ID verification (kept confidential)
- Apply same process to: YouPorn, RedTube, Tube8

### XHamster

- **Content Removal**: https://xhamster.com/content-removal
- Has a specific non-consensual content category
- Can also use the report button on each video page

### XVideos / XNXX

- Use report button on video page, selecting non-consensual content
- Or email dmca@xvideos.com with subject line mentioning non-consensual content
- WGCZ (parent company) processes both DMCA and NCII reports

## Filing Tips

1. **File NCII reports first** where available — they are faster and more private
2. **Then file DMCA** as a secondary enforcement mechanism
3. **Use StopNCII.org early** to create hashes that auto-block on participating platforms
4. **Report the uploader** in addition to the content — platforms may investigate and remove their entire upload history
5. **Check back after 48-72 hours** to verify removal. If not removed, escalate.
6. **Update your tracking spreadsheet** with the report type, date filed, and outcome
