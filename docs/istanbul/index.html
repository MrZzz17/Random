Sunday, April 13
Sultanahmet Classics
9:00 AM
Hagia Sophia
Arrive early to beat the crowds at this 1,500-year-old marvel. Once a church, then a mosque, then a museum, now a mosque again. The massive dome and golden mosaics will take your breath away.
🏛 Must-See📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
10:30 AM
Blue Mosque
Sultan Ahmed Mosque, with its cascading domes and six minarets, is stunning from the outside. Inside, over 20,000 handmade Iznik tiles in blue patterns line the walls. Free entry; headscarves available at the door.
🏛 Landmark📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
11:30 AM
Basilica Cistern
An underground cathedral of 336 marble columns, atmospheric lighting reflected in shallow water, and the mysterious Medusa heads. One of the most dramatic, otherworldly spaces you'll ever see.
📸 Iconic🏛 Must-See
Open in Maps
1:00 PM · Lunch
Seven Hills Restaurant
Rooftop dining with a jaw-dropping panorama of the Blue Mosque and Hagia Sophia. Fresh seafood and Turkish classics while soaking in the most iconic view in the city.
🍽 Rooftop Dining📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
2:30 PM
Topkapi Palace & Harem
The Ottoman sultans' residence for 400 years. Wander through jewel-encrusted rooms, the Imperial Treasury (home to the 86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond), and the legendary Harem quarters.
🏛 Must-See📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
4:30 PM · Afternoon Tea
Hafiz Mustafa 1864
Ottoman-era patisserie with a gorgeous interior. Try the baklava, Turkish delight, and a cup of thick Turkish coffee. The chandeliers and ornate decor make it incredibly photogenic.
🍰 Patisserie📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
💡

Pro tip: Hagia Sophia is free but can have 1-2 hour lines by 10am. Arriving at 9am sharp gets you inside with space to breathe. Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered).

3
Monday, April 14
Bosphorus & Palaces
10:00 AM
Dolmabahce Palace
The most opulent palace in Turkey, where Ottoman meets European baroque. The 36-ton Bohemian crystal chandelier in the throne room alone is worth the trip. Book tickets online in advance.
🏛 Must-See📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
12:30 PM
Ortakoy Mosque & Waterfront
The baroque-style Ortakoy Mosque sits right at the water's edge with the Bosphorus Bridge soaring behind it. One of the most iconic photo compositions in all of Istanbul. Grab a famous Ortakoy kumpir (stuffed baked potato) while you're here.
📸 Iconic🥔 Street Food
Open in Maps
1:30 PM · Lunch
Bebek or Arnavutkoy Waterfront
Continue along the Bosphorus to these charming coastal villages. Elegant wooden Ottoman mansions line the water. Pick a waterfront restaurant for fresh fish with views of the strait.
🍽 Seafood📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
5:30 PM · Sunset
Private Bosphorus Sunset Yacht Cruise
The highlight of the trip. A 2-hour private yacht cruise at golden hour, sailing between Europe and Asia. Watch the city's mosques, palaces, and bridges glow as the sun sets over the strait. Drinks on board, photographer optional.
📸 Unforgettable⛵ Experience
Book Cruise (~$315)
💡

Pro tip: For the yacht cruise, bring a light jacket—it gets breezy on the water at sunset. The best photo moment is passing Maiden's Tower with the Old City skyline behind you.

Tuesday, April 15
Bazaars & Boutiques
10:30 AM
Grand Bazaar
Over 4,000 shops in one of the world's oldest covered markets. Head to the Lantern Courtyard for hand-blown glass lamps glowing in warm colors, the carpet tunnels where sunlight hits vivid kilims, and the hidden tea balcony above the jewelry section. Arrive at 10:30 for the golden skylight effect.
🛍 Shopping📸 Photo Spot🏛 Historic
Open in Maps
12:30 PM
Spice Bazaar (Misir Carsisi)
Mountains of colorful spices, Turkish delight, dried fruits, teas, and saffron. The aromas are intoxicating. The surrounding streets have even better prices—explore the alleys behind the bazaar for local finds.
🛍 Shopping🫖 Treats
Open in Maps
1:30 PM · Lunch
Hamdi Restaurant
Terrace dining overlooking the Golden Horn, Galata Bridge, and the waterfront. Famous for their kebabs and meze. The views from the top-floor terrace are spectacular.
🍽 Turkish Cuisine📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
3:00 PM
Nisantasi Luxury Shopping
Istanbul's most prestigious fashion district. Browse Begum Khan (artisan jewelry in a whimsical emerald boutique), Zadig & Voltaire, and local designer boutiques along Abdi Ipekci Caddesi. Stop for coffee at a chic Nisantasi cafe.
🛍 Luxury Shopping☕ Cafe
Open in Maps
💡

Pro tip: At the Grand Bazaar, never accept the first price—haggling is expected and part of the fun. Start at 40-50% of the asking price. The lantern courtyard photos are best between 10:30am-12:30pm when natural light mixes with the lamps.

5
Wednesday, April 16
Princes' Islands Escape
9:00 AM · Ferry
Kabatas to Buyukada
Catch an early ferry from Kabatas pier (a short walk from Beyoglu). The 60-minute ride across the Sea of Marmara is breathtaking—grab a seat on the upper rear deck for the best views of the receding Istanbul skyline. Tap your Istanbulkart (75 TL one way).
⛴ Ferry Ride📸 Photo Spot
Check Ferry Schedule
10:30 AM
Explore Buyukada by Bike
The largest Princess Island is completely car-free. Rent bikes or hop on the Adabus BA-1 electric shuttle (Great Tour, 60 TL) for a loop around the island. Pine forests, Victorian mansions, lavender gardens, and sea breeze. A total escape from the city.
📸 Photo Spot🚲 Nature
12:00 PM
Aya Yorgi Church Hike
Hike up to the hilltop Greek Orthodox monastery of St. George. The panoramic views from the top—the Sea of Marmara, the other islands, and Istanbul in the distance—are absolutely worth the climb.
📸 Panoramic Views⛪ Historic
Open in Maps
1:30 PM · Lunch
Seafood by the Water
Fresh grilled fish at one of the waterfront restaurants near the main pier. Simple, fresh, and incredibly satisfying after the hike. Try the fried calamari and a cold glass of Efes.
🐟 Seafood
5:30 PM · Sunset Ferry
Return to Istanbul
Catch the late afternoon ferry back. The return trip at sunset, watching Istanbul's skyline light up as you approach, is one of the most magical moments of the whole trip.
📸 Sunset
💡

Pro tip: Go on a weekday (like today, Wednesday) to avoid massive weekend crowds. Bring water, sunscreen, and comfortable shoes for the Aya Yorgi hike. The 9:00-9:45 AM ferries are best for beating the rush.

Thursday, April 17
Colorful Balat & Asian Side
10:00 AM
Balat Neighborhood
The Instagram goldmine of Istanbul. Walk Kiremit Street for the famous rainbow row houses, climb the colorful painted stairs at Incir Agaci Kahvesi, and wander through narrow streets filled with street art, quirky antique shops, and artsy cafes. Every corner is a photo op.
📸 Instagram Heaven🛍 Antiques
Open in Maps
11:30 AM
Coffee at a Balat Cafe
Ducked into a quirky, colorful cafe is a Balat essential. Try Incir Agaci Kahvesi at the top of the painted stairs, or Forno Balat for excellent coffee and pastries in an old brick-oven bakery.
☕ Cafe📸 Photo Spot
1:00 PM · Ferry
Cross to Kadikoy (Asian Side)
Take a ferry from Eminonu to Kadikoy—a quick 20-minute ride across the Bosphorus to Asia. The ferry ride itself is an experience with views of Maiden's Tower and the Old City skyline.
⛴ Ferry Ride
1:30 PM
Kadikoy Market
A vibrant, authentic local food market. Sample olives, fresh cheese, baklava, pomegranate juice, and pickled everything. This is where actual Istanbulites shop—more genuine than the tourist bazaars.
🫒 Market🛍 Local Finds
Open in Maps
3:00 PM
Moda Seaside Promenade
Walk the charming Moda coastal path. Indie boutiques, vintage stores, ice cream shops, and panoramic views of the European skyline from the Asian side. A completely different vibe from the tourist areas.
📸 Photo Spot🛍 Boutiques
Open in Maps
5:00 PM · Sunset
Camlica Hill
The highest point in Istanbul. Panoramic 360-degree views of the entire city, both continents, the Bosphorus, and the Sea of Marmara. At sunset, the view is unreal. The new Camlica Mosque is also stunning.
📸 Panoramic🏛 Mosque
Open in Maps
7:30 PM · Dinner
Ciya Sofrasi
A legendary Kadikoy institution serving traditional Anatolian dishes you won't find anywhere else. Seasonal stews, unique kebabs, and regional specialties from all corners of Turkey. Anthony Bourdain called it a must-visit.
🍽 Legendary
Open in Maps
💡

Pro tip: In Balat, arrive before 11am to get photos of the colorful houses without crowds. The best light for Kiremit Street is mid-morning. For Camlica Hill, a taxi from Kadikoy is easiest (~15 min, cheap).

Friday, April 18
Hammam, Istiklal & Farewell
10:00 AM
Hurrem Sultan Hamam
A 470-year-old Turkish bath built in 1556, sitting between Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque. The Zevk-i Sefa package (80 min, €220) includes a traditional scrub, foam massage, and oil treatment under magnificent Ottoman domes with star-shaped skylights. Pure luxury and renewal.
🛁 Hammam✨ Luxury
Book Hammam
12:00 PM
Istiklal Street Stroll
Istanbul's vibrant main avenue. Ride the nostalgic red tram, browse local designer boutiques and bookshops in the side streets, pop into Cicek Pasaji (Flower Passage) for its ornate glass arcades. The energy here is electric.
🛍 Shopping🚃 Nostalgic Tram
Open in Maps
1:30 PM · Lunch
360 Istanbul
A 360-degree panoramic rooftop restaurant on top of the historic Misir Apartment building. Modern Turkish cuisine and sushi, award-winning cocktails, and views that span the entire city. The space transforms into a club at night.
🍽 Fine Dining📸 360° Views
Reserve a Table
3:00 PM
Last-Minute Treasures
Free time to revisit your favorite spots, pick up last-minute gifts, or explore any places you missed. The streets around Cihangir and Cukurcuma are full of hidden antique shops and design stores.
🛍 Shopping
7:30 PM · Farewell Dinner
Mikla Restaurant
The grand finale. New Anatolian fine dining on the Marmara Pera Hotel rooftop, by celebrity chef Mehmet Gurs. Tasting menus that reinvent Turkish cuisine with Scandinavian precision. The terrace view of the Golden Horn at night is the perfect way to say goodbye to Istanbul.
🍽 Fine Dining📸 Rooftop⭐ Michelin
Open in Maps
💡

Pro tip: Book the hammam in advance—Hurrem Sultan gets fully booked. For Mikla, a reservation is essential (try to book a terrace table). The tasting menu is the way to go for a special last night.
