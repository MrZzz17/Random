9:00
Hagia Sophia
Imagine walking into a building that's been standing for 1,500 years. It was a church, then a mosque, then a museum, and now a mosque again — each empire left its mark. The golden mosaics catch the light in a way that photos can't capture.
🏛 Must-See📷 Instagrammable
📷

Instagrammable: The best shot is from the pathway between Hagia Sophia and Blue Mosque with ornate lamps framing both.

Open in Maps
10:30
Blue Mosque
The reason they call it 'Blue' — over 20,000 handmade tiles covering every surface in hypnotic patterns. Six minarets (most mosques only have one or two). Free entry, headscarves provided at the door. Walk in, look up, and just breathe.
🏛 Landmark📸 Photo Spot
Open in Maps
11:30
Basilica Cistern
An underground palace hidden beneath the city streets. 336 marble columns, moody red lighting reflecting off still water, and two giant Medusa heads no one can fully explain. It feels like walking into a movie set — dark, mysterious, and unforgettable.
📷 Instagrammable🏛 Must-See
📷

Instagrammable: The dramatic lighting changes throughout — the reflections in the shallow water create magical mirror effects. Use portrait mode.

Dolmabahce Palace
If Topkapi was old-money power, Dolmabahce is new-money flex. The sultans built this to show Europe they could out-baroque the best. The 36-ton crystal chandelier — a gift from Queen Victoria — is hanging from the ceiling. Every single room is more extra than the last.
🏛 Must-See📸 Photo Spot

This tiny baroque mosque sits right at the water's edge with the massive Bosphorus Bridge soaring behind — literally THE most iconic Istanbul shot. The vibe here is incredible: street artists, waterfront cafes, and kumpir stands. Kumpir is a giant baked potato stuffed with everything — messy, amazing, a must.
